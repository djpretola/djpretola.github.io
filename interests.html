My personal interests revolve around computers. Since I was young, I have been fascinated by what can be accomplished by them. Since I am a Software Engineer, I have focused on the building of software. I want to build software systems that are effective, reliable and extendible allowing its user to accomplish their own goals.

Engineering is the obvious methodology to accomplish this objective. I view the work of Software Engineers as controlling complexity and specifically maintaining this control through constant change in technology and requirements. Unlike a Mechanical Engineer, whom applies continuous mathematics, physics and materials for mechanical systems; a Software Engineer applies discrete mathematics, logic and electronics for information systems.

Computer Programming is the means to implementing this methodology. There are many programming paradigms, imperative, object oriented, functional to name and few, and all have their strengths and weaknesses when applied. Determining which methodology works the best for a particular set of requirements and containing the ensuing complexity is the job of the Software Engineer. I am actively pursuing knowledge and skills in this domain.

To be more concrete in my interest in computers, I am interested in autonomous systems and the challenges they provide in robotics, artificial intelligence and society as a whole. I like software systems that have the ability to directly effect the world around them, without a direct dependence on people, such as robotics, and SCADA systems. I find software that does more then simply compute data into information and then store that information with the reliance on a person using it to effect the world, fascinating.

Additionally, I am interested in GIS and the ability to model spatial information and gain new insight into the real world. This interest allows me to link the natural world, that I enjoy spending time in, and the world of computing.
